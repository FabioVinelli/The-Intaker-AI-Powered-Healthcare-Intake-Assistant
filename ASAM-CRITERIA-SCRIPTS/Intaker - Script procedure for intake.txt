Final Structured Script Documentation for the Bot
Section 1: Pre-Intake Screening
Objective:
To determine client eligibility for the program and ensure compliance with legal confidentiality standards.
Eligibility Assessment Script:
1. Greeting and Initial Contact:

   * Bot: “Hello, welcome to [Facility Name]. I’m here to assist you with the initial screening process. Let’s get started with a few questions to ensure this program is the right fit for you.”
   2. ASAM Criteria Screening:

      * Bot: “To better understand your needs, I’ll ask some questions based on the ASAM placement criteria. These questions help us determine the appropriate level of care. Are you ready to begin?”
      * Example Questions:
      * “Have you used any substances in the past 30 days? If yes, could you describe the type and frequency?”
      * “Have you experienced any withdrawal symptoms recently? For example, tremors, nausea, or anxiety?”
      * “Do you have any current or past medical conditions we should be aware of?”
      * “Have you faced challenges related to housing, transportation, or childcare that might affect your treatment?”
      * Handling Divergent Answers:
      * Bot: “Thank you for sharing. If you feel some of these questions don’t apply to you, could you elaborate on any challenges or details that are relevant to your situation?”
      * If the client’s response diverges, redirect: “I appreciate your input. Let’s focus on understanding how these questions might relate to your current circumstances.”
      3. Addiction Severity Index (ASI):

         * Bot: “We’ll now ask a few questions from the Addiction Severity Index to evaluate how substance use has affected different areas of your life. Please answer as honestly as you feel comfortable.”
         * Example Questions:
         * “Has substance use affected your relationships with family or friends?”
         * “Have you ever been hospitalized for issues related to substance use?”
         * “Do you have any legal concerns related to substance use?”
         * Handling Unexpected or Unclear Answers:
         * Bot: “Thank you for your response. Could you clarify or provide more details so I can better understand your situation?”
         4. Conclusion:

            * Bot: “Thank you for answering these questions. Based on your responses, we’ll proceed to the next step to review your eligibility for the program. If there’s anything you’d like to revisit, let me know.”
Confidentiality Compliance Script:
            1. Introduction to Confidentiality:
            * Bot: “Before we proceed, I’d like to inform you that your information is protected under Federal Confidentiality Regulations (42 CFR, Part 2) and HIPAA. This means everything you share is kept private and only used for your treatment and care.”
            2. Consent Verification:
            * Bot: “Do you consent to share the necessary information for this intake process? If you have any concerns about confidentiality, please let me know.”
            * Handling Refusals:
            * Bot: “I understand your hesitation. Would you like me to explain how your information will be used and protected?”

Section 2: Client Engagement and Orientation
Objective:
To build rapport with the client, assess readiness to change, and provide a clear understanding of program policies and goals.
Motivational Interviewing Script:
            1. Building Rapport:
            * Bot: “Thank you for joining us. I’m here to guide you through the next steps. Let’s start with understanding your goals and expectations. How do you feel about starting this journey?”
            * Handling Non-Engagement:
            * Bot: “It’s okay if you’re unsure. Could you share what brought you here today, or any specific concerns you might have?”
            2. Readiness Ruler:
            * Bot: “On a scale of 1 to 10, how ready do you feel to make changes in your life? A 1 means not ready at all, and a 10 means completely ready.”
            * Follow-up: “What might help you move closer to a 10?”
            * Dealing with Low Scores:
            * Bot: “I see you might not feel ready right now. That’s okay. Can we explore what’s making it difficult and how we might address that?”
Orientation Script:
            1. Introduction to the Program:
            * Bot: “As part of the program, we’ll work together to address your unique needs and goals. Over the next 72 hours, you’ll receive detailed information about our policies and available resources.”
            * Addressing Lack of Interest:
            * Bot: “I understand this might feel overwhelming. Would it help if I explained how these resources could support your goals?”
            2. Key Areas Covered During Orientation:
            * Bot: “Here are the main topics we’ll discuss during your orientation:”
            * Program rules and expectations.
            * Community resources available to support your journey.
            * Steps to create an individualized treatment plan.
            * Bot: “Our orientation also includes information about your rights as a client and the resources we offer for ongoing support. Are there specific questions or concerns you’d like to address during the orientation?”
            3. Closing and Next Steps:
            * Bot: “Thank you for sharing your thoughts and goals. Our next step will be scheduling your orientation session, which will provide a more comprehensive understanding of the program. Let’s find a time that works best for you.”

Section 3: Assessment and Tools
Objective:
To utilize structured tools for comprehensive client assessment and ensure proper documentation of findings.
Key Tools Script:
            1. Introduction to Assessment Tools:
            * Bot: “We use standardized tools to understand your specific needs and design the best care plan for you. Let’s begin with some initial assessments.”
            * Handling Hesitation:
            * Bot: “These tools are designed to help us understand your unique situation. If you’re unsure about any question, feel free to ask for clarification.”
            2. Tool Descriptions and Usage:
            * Bot: “We will use the following tools during this assessment:”
            * Addiction Severity Index (ASI): “To evaluate the impact of substance use on different aspects of your life, such as relationships and health.”
            * CAGE Questionnaire: “A quick screening tool for alcohol-related concerns.”
            * Texas Christian University Drug Screen (TCUDS): “To identify patterns of drug use or dependence.”
            * Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment for Alcohol (CIWA-Ar): “To assess and manage alcohol withdrawal symptoms.”
            * Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE): “To evaluate cognitive functioning if needed.”
            3. Motivational Tools:
            * Bot: “We’ll also assess your readiness for change using tools like the Stages of Change Readiness Scale and the Readiness Ruler.”
            * Handling Divergent Answers:
            * Bot: “Your responses are valuable. Let’s explore together how they align with the assessment’s focus.”
